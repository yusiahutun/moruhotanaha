Tanzanian billionaire Rostam Aziz has been granted a license to construct a cooking gas plant at the Dongo Kundu special economic zone in Mombasa county.

The Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority (EPRA) gave the green light to Aziz who owns Taifa Gas to also build storage facilities at the Mombasa port.

The entry of Taifa gas into the Kenyan Liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) gas market is expected to provide stiff competition to other players in the industry and could pave the way for lower cooking gas prices.

Taifa Gas plant to create 90,000 jobs

Over 90,000 Kenyans will benefit from job opportunities in the coastal region through the initiation of the project which will cost KSh 16 billion.

The project's launch was presided over by President William Ruto on Friday, February 24.

"The project we are starting today doe not only aim to make LPG affordable , but to provide employment and income to many families in this country," Taifa Gas managing director Veneranda Masoum said.

According to the head of commercial communication Angela Boke, already residents near the construction site of the 30-acre project had been involved in preparing the land for construction.

"We plan to open warehouses in 10 ocations in the country. There we will look for land and the residents are helping us to prepare those areas," she said.

Who is Rostam Aziz?

The 58-year-old businessman is an economist and former member of parliament for Igunga constituency.

The Henley & Partners Africa wealth report 2022 indicated he is the only dollar billionaire in East Africa.

In 2015, Forbes ranked him among the 50 richest Africans with an estimated net worth of $900 million (KSh 114.5 billion).

The tycoon obtained his bachelor's in economics degree from Exeter University in England.

Other businesses owned by Aziz

Besides his investments in the energy sector, Aziz also has interests in telecoms, mining, agriculture, media, real estate, port facilities and services.

Vodacom Tanzania, Tigo and Zantel

According to Forbes, Aziz controlled 18% of Vodacom Tanzania, the East African country's leading cellular network company with over 16.7 million subscribers as per The East African.

Vodacom Tanzania leads the telecoms market with a 31% share.

In May 2014, he sold 17% of the company to Vodacom Group of South Africa for an estimated $250 million (KSh 31.8 billion).

The Citizen reported that Aziz and Axian Group of Madagascar acquired Tigo and Zantel at $100 million (KSh 12.7 billion).

Tigo is ranked second in terms of market share with 27% in Tanzania.

2. Caspian Mining

Aziz has invested in the extractives sector through Caspian Mining which was founded in 1987 according to its website.

The company deals in grade control, concrete works, earthworks, buildings, drills and blasts.

Its revenue is estimated at over $6 million (KSh 763.5 million) according to Rockreach.

3. Ace Leather Tanzania

The business magnate also has interests in the leather industry through Ace Leather Tanzania.

The company invested TSh 50 billion in a tannery opened by former president John Magufuli. It is operated by Taifa Leather Company Limited.

William Ruto pledges to remove cooking gas taxes

President William Ruto vowed to ensure every household has access to cooking gas to reduce wood fuel use.

During the launch of Taifa Gas at Dogo Kundu, the head of state said the government would remove all taxes levied on gas.

Ruto said eliminating wood fuel would also help Kenya restore its environment by reducing the number of trees being cut for wood fuel.

He tasked Energy Cabinet Secretary Davis Chirchir to ensure cooking gas is made available to Kenyan households at a subsidised price within three years.
