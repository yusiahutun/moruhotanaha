Kenya to Raise KSh 70b from Infrastructure Bonds Amid Growing Domestic Debt

 Posted on August 3, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  548 words  |  Larita Shotwell
  • The Kenyan government has advertised the issuance of infrastructure bonds worth KSh 70 billion to raise money to fund development
  • The minimum amount of money that can be invested into the bonds was reduced from KSh 100,000 and now stands at KSh 50,000
  • The infrastructure bond, with a lifespan of 8.5 years, will be tax-free, and the coupon rate will be market-determined

Elijah Ntongai, a journalist at TUKO.co.ke, has more than three years of financial, business, and technology research expertise, providing insights into Kenyan and global economic trends.

Kenya has announced plans to raise KSh 70 billion through the issuance of infrastructure bonds in February 2024.

The 8.5-year security will be offered as a tax-exempt bond, mirroring other debt instruments issued to support infrastructure projects in Kenya.

The Central Bank of Kenya released the "Prospectus for the Government of Kenya Infrastructure Bond Offer," detailing the terms and conditions.

This will increase the domestic debt owed by the government, which, as per the January 19 CBK disclosures, stood at KSh 5.04 trillion.

What is a bond?

According to CBK, treasury bonds are a secure, medium- to long-term investment that typically offer you interest payments every six months throughout the bond’s maturity.

A bond is a unique agreement, similar to a loan, between a borrower, in this case, the Kenyan government, and a lender (the bondholder), in this case, investors who will purchase the infrastructure bond.

The infrastructure bond is issued by the Kenyan government to fund development projects.

Terms and conditions of the Infrastructure Bonds

The securities will be an 8.5-year amortised bond, whose coupon rate will be market-determined.

The minimum amount of money that can be invested into the bonds was reduced from KSh 100,000 and now stands at KSh 50,000 to increase the number of Kenyans participating in the securities market.

How to invest in the infrastructure bond

The bond will be listed on the Nairobi Securities Exchange, and only investors with active CSD accounts will be able to make purchases.

CBK reserves the right to reject and accept bids without giving any reason.

What is an amortised bond?

An amortised bond is a type of bond where the principal amount, the initial investment paid by the investor, is gradually paid off over the life of the bond.

In Kenya's case, the redemption date for the infrastructure bond advertised here will be August 2023. However, interest payments will begin in August 2024.

In other related news, Kenya's initial Eurobond debt at issuance was KSh 697.7 billion, but it has escalated to KSh 1.117 trillion due to the depreciation of the local currency.
