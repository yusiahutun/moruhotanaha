Amanda Holden's co-star was told not to meet her and Alan Carr before filming BBC show

 Posted on October 3, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  953 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Amanda and Alan's Italian Job star was told not to meet the duo before filming the BBC Show.

Fans have watched BGT judge Amanda Holden and comedian Alan Carr team up to transform derelict buildings purchased for one euro into stunning properties. They have appeared in two series so far which were filmed in Tuscany and Siciliy.

Whilst trying their hand at everything, the pair also drafted in local tradespeople to show them the ropes before the resonated building goes on sale. Scott Thompson, a Sicily-based project manager and maker, moved to Italy in 2007 in search for sunny holiday escape and ended up purchasing a house for cheap, just like Amanda and Alan do in the hit BBC series.

He has recently appeared on the Soap From The Box podcast with host Lee Salisbury to discuss his time on the show and meeting Amanda and Alan for the first time. He said: "Meeting them for the first time was really funny. So that first scene in the first season where I come in that's actually where I met them.

"The production team were very convinced that I shouldn't meet them before live on camera for the first time but we had to do two takes. Amanda and Alan had been there doing some cleaning and then I came in. I was so nervous that I leant in to kiss Amanda on the cheek and I kind of headbutted her."

Despite appearing on both series, Scott revealed that he took some convincing to sign up for the show. He explained: "I don't think I was convinced straight away because I had quite a bit of stuff on. I think I took a bit of convincing to do the first one. I don't really watch much TV so I didn't know anything about it, I hadn't heard anything about it. So I wasn't sure at first but i'm glad I did because it's such good fun."

However, things didn't get off to the best start as he started off his new adventure abroad 17 yers ago. Scott said: "I was a builder in Manchester doing basement conversations. It was always raining and I was underground. I really needed a sunny escape and I ended up in a tiny little town in Sicily with the intention of saying for three months and renovating a little house, a bit of a summer project, and moving back to England.
