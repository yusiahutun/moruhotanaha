Mia Khalifa takes to catwalk in UK and poses for nude snaps in 'house from 1400s'

 Posted on May 23, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  480 words  |  Martina Birk

Mia Khalifa has returned to the UK once again - and is now officially a catwalk model.

The Lebanese-America star, 30, moved into the fashion world after quitting adult films and launched her own brand, Sheytan, earlier this year.

She returned to Britain this time for London Fashion Week and took to the runway kitted out in clothes from London-based brand KNWLS. But that isn't all she has been getting up to back in Blighty.

Taking to Instagram yesterday (Monday, September 18) she shared snaps of the traditional English home she is staying in. "This is my idea of a perfect house," she wrote in one post.

In another she said she was "in a house from the 1400s". It is unclear exactly where she is staying, although she has previously declared her love for the seaside town of Whitstable in Kent. She once said in a social media post: "Whitstable > Miami."

Mia has been using the old house as a quaint backdrop for saucy images she is sharing with fans. In one mirror selfie on Instagram, the home's wooden beams are visible behind her as she poses in only a racy green bikini.

On X (formerly Twitter) she went as far as to share a fully nude image in front of the home's fireplace. Only her hair and a tactically placed knee were protecting her modesty.

She captioned the image with lyrics from Arctic Monkeys tune Fireside, writing: "I thought I was yours forever. Or maybe I was mistaken. But I just cannot manage to make it through the day. Without thinking of you lately." The post also included a link to the website where she flogs "safe for work, but spicy" snaps to fans for $8.40 a month.

One follower said in reply to the picture: "It looks like you really enjoy to relax." Another wrote: "Mia being an Arctic Monkeys fan is the best thing ever." While a third said: "She’s a hottie."

Mia has visited the UK on a number of occasions, most recently in May this year when she gave a speech at Oxford University. "The imposter syndrome was REAL," she said in a social media post at the time.

Most of the time she stays in Whitstable. After one recent visit she told followers she was "heartbroken" to be leaving the sleepy seaside town and jetting off back to the US.
