Murdered wife of 'possessed' California gunman pictured

 Posted on February 2, 2024  |  6 minutes  |  1222 words  |  Aldo Pusey

The wife of the Californian gunman who he killed before murdering five people and injuring ten more in a killing spree on Tuesday has been identified. 

The body of Barbara Glisan, 38, was found beneath the floorboards of the home she shared with Kevin Neal in Rancho Tehama, California, on Wednesday. 

A day earlier, Neal, 44, was killed by police at the local elementary school where he had crashed his pick-up truck and opened fire on classrooms after killing five people in cold drive-by's through the town and on the street where he lived. 

He is believed to have murdered Glisan on Monday after a domestic violence incident which police were never called to. Neighbors who are still reeling from the killings on Tuesday revealed the argument after Neal was brought down. 

As his wife was identified for the first time on Thursday, the gunman's tearful older sister began to shed light on his 'deteriorated' mental health and the state of mind which led him to carry out the heinous attack. 

Police believe Barbara Glisan, 38, was killed by her husband Kevin Neal, 44, and buried beneath the floorboards of their home before he launched his shooting spree in Rancho Tehama, California, on Tuesday 

She described him as 'possessed' and 'demonic', telling NBC news in an emotional interview how she had watched his health decline gradually over the last year. 

'His mental condition continued to deteriorate, and you know as a family you want to be supportive and you want to love them — and you know, he just continued to devolve and descend into conspiracy theories and hallucinations and delusions of grandeur,' Sheridan Orr, Neal's sister who lives in Cary, North Carolina, said.  

Though she had not seen him for seven years, Orr, who lives in Cary, North Carolina, spoke with her brother on the phone and noticed that his health began to decline last October.

In their more recent conversations, she said his condition had become so grave she no longer recognized the brother she grew up with. 

'There was no incline of that child who we loved who could sing opera and crack jokes. It just wasn't there anymore.' 

Orr said her brother had struggled with mental health his whole life and was a 'sensitive' child who felt things 'more acutely' than others. 

She believes that if he'd been given the help he so desperately needed, the attack could have been prevented. 

It is not known how long Glisan and Neal (right in a previous mugshot) had been married 

Sheridan Orr, the shooter's older sister, tearfully told on Thursday how he had struggled with mental health problems his whole life 

Orr, who lives in Cary, North Carolina, sobbed as she told how she had not seen her brother for seven years but spoke to him regularly on the phone and worried about his deteriorating condition 

'There were so many triggers, I have no idea and I would pay anything to know and to know why he would do this and hurt those people,' she sobbed. 

'We cannot help but feel that a) he needed better mental health care and we needed a way to get that for him because that is something that would have changed this story, and b) he had no business with guns.' 

Sheridan said the country was compelled to have a 'conversation' on the issue to of gun control after so many tragedies involving mentally ill mass murderers. 

'I don't think I could bear another family sitting in my chair talking about this or those people who got calls that their friends and family were victims. we have to do something, we have to do better.' 

Neal used a semi-automatic rifle and two handguns to carry out the killings. None of the weapons were registered to him but police have not yet revealed who they belonged to. 

Neal's rampage began on Bobcat Lane, the street where he lived with Glisan, then carried on through the town towards the school 

Police found Glisan's body beneath the floorboards of the home she shared with Neal (above) on Wednesday 

On his way towards the school, the gunman shot at random at passing cars including this Ford truck. A woman and her child were inside and both were hit by his bullets and were taken to hospital 

The boarded up Rancho Tehama Elementary School on Wednesday. By the time Neal arrived there, all of the children had been ushered inside by the secretary who heard the shots coming further away 

One of the bullet holes left at the elementary school on Tuesday after the shooting 

Alejandro Hernandez, six, was the only child who was shot at the school. He was hit twice by bullets and is recovering in hospital 

He had been banned from owning weapons by a judge after being charged with felony assault with a deadly weapon in January for stabbing his neighbor. 

At the time of Tuesday's killing, he was out on bond. 

It is not known how long Neal and Glisan were married or how long they had lived together in California. Details of how he killed her have not been shared by police but her death was the first in his murderous, two-day rampage. 

On Tuesday at 7.52am, he opened fire on Bobcat Lane, the street where they lived. Neighbors have since told how he had long been fighting with others on the street and that he fired his guns at anti-social hours. 

After firing his first shots, Neal got into someone else's car and began to drive towards Rancho Tehama. 

He shot three people along the way from inside the truck including a woman and her child who were in their own vehicle when his bullets pierced their windshield and windows. 

Neal used two stolen trucks in his rampage and crashed both of them. Above, one of the vehicles 

FBI agents are still working through the seven crime scenes Neal left on his deadly rampage 

Shell casings on the road left by Neal's semi-automatic rifle and handguns 

Neal then crashed his vehicle and stole another to take him to Rancho Tehama Elementary School where parents were dropping their children off for the day. 

Fresh details have now emerged of how a quick-thinking school secretary put the school in lockdown after hearing some of his earlier shots on the road leading towards the school campus.

Without waiting for the police go-ahead - which is procedure in lockdown scenarios - she ushered all of the 100 school children into classrooms before Neal arrived. 

By the time he rammed his truck into the school gates, there were no longer any children in the playground. 

He tried to get inside the school but was unsuccessful so started firing shots through classroom windows from the playground. 

For six minutes he fired his gun as the children cowered on the ground with their teachers and parents. 

One child was shot twice - in the chest and the leg - by bullets which pierced through the classroom window. They are in hospital. 

None of those who were killed were children.

Police arrived soon thereafter and returned fire as Neal shot at them. He died at the scene.
