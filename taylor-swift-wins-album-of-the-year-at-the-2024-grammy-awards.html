Taylor Swift Wins Album Of The Year At The 2024 Grammy Awards

 Posted on January 1, 0001  |  42 minutes  |  8738 words  |  Martina Birk

LOS ANGELES (CelebrityAccess) — Taylor Swift made history on Sunday when she won Album of the Year for her latest album “Midnights” becoming the first artist to win the coveted award four times.

Swift was previously tied with fellow music legends Frank Sinatra, Paul Simon, and Stevie Wonder, who each have three Albums of the Year awards to their names.

Along with Album of the Year, Swift also took home trophies for Best Pop Vocal Album, but lost out to Miley Cyrus‘ “Flowers” for Record of the Year.

Victoria Monét, who stunned fans with her debut studio album “Jaguar II” last year, was named the Best New Artist for 2023.

“What Was I Made For?” Billie Eilish‘s contribution to The Barbie Movie soundtrack earned her and her brother and songwriting partner Finneas O’Connell, earned them the Grammy for Song of the Year.

Killer Mike provided drama for the Grammys this year, winning the trophy for Best Rap Performance for his hit “Scientists & Engineers,” featuring André 3000, Future, and Eryn Allen Kane but he also left the ceremony in handcuffs after he was allegedly involved in an physical altercation during the pre-show.

Highlights for the 2024 Grammys included a performance from Joni Mitchell, who took the Grammy stage for the first time and who also won the award for Best Folk Album for “Joni Mitchell at Newport.”

Tracy Chapman joined Luke Combs on stage for a rendition of her hit “Fast Car” which Luke helped to bring back the charts when he recorded his own version for his 2023 album Gettin’ Old. Combs’ take on the song peaked at #2 on the Billboard Country chart but also broke through contemporary charts and landed at #1 on the Hot Country Songs chart.

Other highlights included Taylor Swift, who used the bully platform provided by her Grammy wins to tease her forthcoming album “The Tortured Poets Department” which is due out on April 19th.
