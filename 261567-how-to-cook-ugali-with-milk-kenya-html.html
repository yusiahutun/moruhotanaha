Kenyan ugali recipe

Kenyan ugali can be served with vegetables, sauce, stew and even acrid milk/mala.

  • Heat the milk in a heavy duty sufuria and bring it to boil.
  • Scoop a modest bunch of flour and add carefully into the bubbling milk. Leave the milk to heat up some more until the point when it froths and nearly floods from the sufuria. This keeps up the temperature of the milk at breaking point. If this is not done, you will end up with a low milk temperature which results in under-cooked ugali.
  • Utilize the cooking stick to mix. Keep adding flour and mixing until the porridge mixture changes into a strong hard mass.
  • After every addition of flour, leave for a 'bit' time, so the cold flour picks up the temperature and finds warmth. You can cover the sufuria to build up more heat.
  • When mixing, you will need more strength as the mass solidifies further. Thus balance and grasp of the Sufuria and cooking stick are very important at this stage. Fundamental. While stirring press/spread the ugali against the sufuria walls continually while blending. It expels bumps. Exceptionally KEY. Stirring alone creates uneven ugali or porridge.
  • Cover the sufuria when the mass is sufficiently hard as wanted for a 'bit' time to enable warmth to work inside for a more intensive cooking. Build the temperature.
  • Watch out for the inner parts of the sufuria particularly the base. As you mix, the insides get a film of the flour. As the base, partly turns golden, or a dull shade of darker the ugali nears cooking.
  • Allow it to simmer for a moment. Then, using the cooking stick, bring the ugali into a dome shape. Your ugali with milk is now ready.
  • Topple the sufuria on a spotless plate and have your ugali. Keep secured until eating time to keep up the heat however much as could reasonably be expected.
  • Serve while still hot.
