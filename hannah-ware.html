Early Life

Hannah Rose Ware, popularly known as Hannah Ware was born on December 8, 1982, in Hammersmith, London, England, in the United Kingdom. She is the daughter of John Ware who was a BBC Panorama reporter and her mother Helena who was a social worker. Her parents divorced when she was 12 years old and she lived with her mother who raised her along with her younger sister Jessie Ware who is currently a musician. As a kid, Hannah attended the Alleyn’s School which is located in Dulwich. She then went to the Lee Strasberg Theatre and Film Institute located in New York, from where she studied acting and majored in it.

Hannah Ware Career

Hannah made her career debut when she was seen in her first film Cop Out which was released in 2010. In 2011 and 2012, she played the role of Emma Kane, who is the estranged addict daughter of Tom Kane who is a Chicago mayor, in the series named Boss. In 2013, she was a part of the main cast of the ABC drama series titled Betrayal. She also appeared in Spike Lee’s thriller film titled Old boy. In 2015, she played the role of the main cast in the action film Hitman: Agent 47.
