Who is Ari Melber?

Ari Naftali Melber is an acclaimed media personality, serving as an attorney, writer, and TV journalist. He was born to Barbara and Daniel Melber on 31st March 1980 in Seattle, Washington. He has one sibling.

The TV journalist went to Garfield High School in Seattle, Washington. After graduation, he joined the University of Michigan for a degree in political science. He graduated in 2002. Later, he joined Cornell Law School and graduated in 2009 with a J.D.

He hosted The Point, co-hosted The Cycle on MSNBC, and was a substitute host for The Rachel Maddow show. Today, Ari is the chief legal correspondent for MSNBC Live and the legal analyst for Today Show and NBC News. As a host, he is famous for:

  • All in with Chris Hayes
