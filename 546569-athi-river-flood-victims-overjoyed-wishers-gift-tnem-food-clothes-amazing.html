Athi River Flood Victims Overjoyed after Well-wishers Gift Them Food, Clothes: &quot;This is Amazing&quot;

 Posted on January 1, 0001  |  2 minutes  |  398 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon
  • Eric Omondi went back to Athi River, where he rescued people who were marooned in an estate full of floodwater
  • When Omondi responded to the distress call in the estate, he found people on top of roofs stranded, and he lowered them and helped to paddle them to safety
  • After five days, Omondi went back to the estate accompanied by a well-wisher who sell mitumba clothes and presented much-needed goodies to the people

Athi River flood victims could hardly contain their excitement when they saw comedian Eric Omondi visit them bearing goodies.

Comedian Omondi hires boat to help people

Omondi first interacted with the flood victims when they got stranded in an estate that had flooded and soaked all their belongings, rendering them helpless.

In another story, the talented comedian Omondi led the efforts to build an ark to assist flood victims in Kenya.

Accompanied by a dedicated team of carpenters, Eric actively participated in the ark's assembly, which is nearing completion.

The words 'Sisi Kwa Sisi' adorned the ark, emphasising unity and collective action.

The ark project, powered by team Sisi Kwa Sisi, aims to deliver essential supplies to those affected by the devastating floods.

Omondi criticised the government's response to the crisis, highlighting the urgent need for citizen-led initiatives.
