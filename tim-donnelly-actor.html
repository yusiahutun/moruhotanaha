Professional Career

Tim Donnelly made his film debut in 1957 with “Baby Face Nelson.” He has appeared in a number of other films and television shows throughout his career, including “The Virginian,” “Hawaii Five-O,” “Adam-12,” “The Secret of Santa Vittoria,” and “The Legend of Jesse James.”

He also appeared in five episodes of “Dragnet,” where he was mentored by Jack Webb. Despite his extensive filmography, it was his role as a cheerful, carefree Chet Kelly in the NBC drama series “Emergency!” that pushed him to fame.
