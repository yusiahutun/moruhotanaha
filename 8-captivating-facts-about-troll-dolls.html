The Origins of Troll Dolls

Troll dolls, also known as Dam dolls or Good Luck Trolls, were originally created in 1959 by Danish woodcutter Thomas Dam. He made the first troll doll as a gift for his daughter using wood scraps. The doll’s unique appearance and charm quickly gained popularity, leading to the mass production of troll dolls.

Troll Dolls and Their Wild Hair

One of the most distinctive features of troll dolls is their unruly and colorful hair. The original troll dolls had hair made of sheep wool, but later versions were made with synthetic fibers. The hair could be brushed, styled, and even interchanged between different troll dolls, adding to their appeal.

The Troll Doll Craze in the 1960s

In the 1960s, troll dolls became a global phenomenon. They were originally marketed as a toy for children, but soon their popularity spread to people of all ages. The dolls were often used as collectibles and even became fashionable accessories, with people proudly displaying their troll doll collections.

Troll Dolls as Good Luck Charms

Many people believed that troll dolls brought good luck and protection. People would keep troll dolls in their homes, cars, and even workplaces as a symbol of good fortune. Their small size and endearing features made them perfect for carrying around as a lucky charm.

Troll Dolls in Pop Culture

Troll dolls have made appearances in various forms of popular culture over the years. They have been featured in movies, TV shows, and even music videos. In 2016, an animated film called “Trolls” was released, reigniting the interest in these iconic toys.

Collecting Rare Troll Dolls

Like any collectible item, rare troll dolls can be a valuable commodity. Some troll dolls, especially those from the 1960s and early editions, can fetch high prices in the collector’s market. Special editions, limited releases, and unique variations are highly sought after by avid collectors.

The Evolution of Troll Dolls

Over the years, troll dolls have gone through various transformations. From the traditional appearance with a potbelly, big eyes, and a wide smile, to modern versions featuring different outfits, accessories, and even themed characters like sports trolls or holiday trolls. The evolution of troll dolls continues to captivate collectors and enthusiasts.

The Enduring Popularity of Troll Dolls
