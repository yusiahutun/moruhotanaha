Timeless icons from Hollywood's Golden Age are seen letting their hair down at the beach and poolside in vintage photos. 

Marylin Monroe, Raquel Welch, Elizabeth Taylor, Sean Connery, Elvis Presley and more can be seen frolicking in the sunshine in the photo set exclusively obtained by DailyMail.com. 

Veteran art collector Klaus Moeller shared the images with DailyMail.com and said he is especially fond of this beach series because they capture a fun and playful side to these beloved celebrities. 

Moeller, CEO of Globe Entertainment and Media, acquired some 25 million archives dating back to the 1930's and admits he still has countless of catalogues to go through.

'How fun, I thought to myself, to find all these images of these iconic entertainers basking in the sun. You can't help but smile looking at them,' the art enthusiast told DailyMail.com.

Many of the images were taken by the renowned photographer Frank Worth who had close relationships with his subjects including Marilyn Monroe, Raquel Welch, Elizabeth Taylor and James Dean. 

These friendships made it possible for Worth to visit classic film sets, such as A Rebel Without A Cause and The Seven Year Itch, where the New York photographer captured some of his most memorable images including the stunning Elizabeth Taylor wearing a white bikini top while leaning against a table on the set of Giant.

Most of the photos taken by Worth were never shown to the public. Even in poverty, the photographer refused to sell his work out of loyalty to his famous friends. 

It wasn't until his death in 2000, that his cousin discovered 10,000 negatives in his apartment.

When not scouring through his millions of archives, Moeller best describes himself as a big kid with an even bigger heart. He can be found at various non-profit charity events where he helps raise funds with his art collections.

For the last 30 years, Moeller had created and supported several orphanages around the world, including a school for handicapped and blind children, and a center for street children in Thailand.

Most recently, German-born Moeller who grew up in Portugal and now resides in Southern California, put on his annual, three-day LA Comic-Con attended by over 150,000 devoted fans. LA Comic Con is the biggest multimedia pop culture event in the City of Angels.
