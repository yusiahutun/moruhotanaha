Here Are the Lyrics to Riley Green's 'I Wish Grandpas Never Died'

 Posted on March 29, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  594 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Riley Green lists his two grandfathers as co-writers on "I Wish Grandpas Never Died" because the song is that personal. Lyrically the nostalgic country ballad laments all the good things you miss in life: old dogs, first kisses, manners and quality time with your grandfather.

Green, a 2019 Taste of Country RISERS artist, has performed the song live a few times. Fan video caught fire, so his label team decided to switch radio singles. "In Love by Now" was looking like a hit, but it was impossible to ignore the power of a song nearly everyone can relate to. Talking to Taste of Country, Green shared the impact his two grandpas had on him and his music.

"My Grandaddy Buford was the country guy," Green tells Taste of Country. "He was really into like Merle Haggard, Roy Acuff, Hank WIlliams and ... that was what I learned to sing and play. I never sang when I played guitar until my grandaddy started pushing me and me and him would sit around and he'd fiddle on the harmonica a little bit. That was where I got my traditional roots."

Buford Green died nearly a decade ago. Lendon Bonds (who inspired the fishing verse) died in 2018, but got to see his grandson sing at the Grand Ole Opry. This song will be included on Green's upcoming Different 'Round Here album, to be released on Sept. 20.
