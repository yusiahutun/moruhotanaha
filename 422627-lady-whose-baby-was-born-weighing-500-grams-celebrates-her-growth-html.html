Mum with Baby Born Weighing Just 500gms Celebrates Her Successful Growth

 Posted on July 20, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  736 words  |  Aldo Pusey
  • Josephine Kagendo knows just too well the pain and patience of watching a premature baby go through the assiduous process of development
  • Six years ago, she gave birth to a baby who weighed a mere 500 grams but has seen her grow through the milestones to date
  • She now uses her story to inspire other mothers with such cases to hold onto hope that things will be better in due course

Six years ago, Josephine Kagendo's pregnancy journey was nipped in the bud when she went into labour earlier than her expected due date.

It was a delivery she had not envisioned, but then doctors took the risk and delivered a premature baby girl weighing a light 500 grams.

Speaking exclusively to TUKO.co.ke's Hillary Lisimba, Josephine shared the gripping story of her daughter's brush with death, and the milestones she's since put on over the years.

Painstaking Journey

As much as both mother and child were alive, the painstaking journey to recovery, especially for the baby, began as doctors meticulously fought tooth and nail to save her.

For three months, Josephine looked at her baby through the glass walls on the little cubicle that had become her new home, expecting the worst but hoping for the best.

"As at the time of being born, her eyes weren't even fully developed, and she was so small that a spoonful of liquid food took her for two days. You could nearly see through her, and the veins were the size of a needle in diameter," she remembered.

According to her, critical counselling prepared her for the worst as she was asked to enjoy every minute with the baby and not worry about the next.

Hope, and Lessons

After a never-ending three months, the professional model was allowed to carry the baby home but had to continue offering critical care until she was fully developed.

It is a period within which Josephine reveals she prayed countless times to God and promised that if He saved her daughter's life, she would offer a home to another disadvantaged baby.

As time went by, baby Chloe Zawadi Stegeman began fighting through the hurdles and jostled her way into a fully formed baby like she never spent a day under critical care.

"She is a very beautiful, intelligent and creative girl right now who is turning six on October 29. I see her and I see God," she emotionally expressed.

Given that God had kept His end of the bargain, Josephine and her husband are currently on the list of adopting an eight-year-old mixed-race African American whom they hope to bring home and show love.

Child Ambassador

It is against this backdrop that Josephine has taken it upon herself to be a child ambassador who advocates for their welfare.

Her focus, she says, is on what is best for the child, from health to mental stability.

She recently registered Dream International, a non-profit organization whose focus is on helping kids get help from educational support to medical aid.

"I want to reach out to many and allow God to use me as His vessel, especially those I can inspire and talk to about my situation openly without shame," she concluded.

Josephine explained that she suffers what is medically known as Placental Insufficiency, which occurs when a woman's placenta does not develop properly.
