Biography TimeLine

David Ortiz, also known as “Big Papi,” is a retired Dominican-American professional baseball player who played for the Boston Red Sox in Major League Baseball (MLB). He is considered one of the greatest designated hitters in MLB history and helped lead the Red Sox to three World Series championships. Here is a detailed biography timeline of David Ortiz:

  • 1975: David Ortiz was born on November 18, in Santo Domingo, Dominican Republic.
  • 1992: David was signed as an amateur free agent by the Seattle Mariners.
  • 1996: David made his MLB debut with the Minnesota Twins.
  • 2002: David was traded to the Boston Red Sox.
  • 2003: David helped lead the Red Sox to the American League Championship Series (ALCS), but the team lost to the New York Yankees in a dramatic seven-game series.
  • 2004: David had a career-best season and helped lead the Red Sox to their first World Series championship in 86 years, breaking the “Curse of the Bambino.”
  • 2007: David won his first Silver Slugger award and helped lead the Red Sox to their second World Series championship in four years.
  • 2013: David won his third World Series championship with the Red Sox and was named the World Series Most Valuable Player (MVP).
  • 2016: David announced that he would retire at the end of the season.
  • 2016: David played his final game on October 2, finishing his career with 541 home runs and 1,768 runs batted in (RBIs).
  • 2017: David was shot in the Dominican Republic and underwent several surgeries to recover. He later announced that he would return to work as a baseball analyst for Fox Sports.

David Ortiz was known for his powerful hitting, clutch performances in meaningful games, and leadership in the Red Sox clubhouse. He also became a beloved figure in Boston. He was recognized for his charitable work, including creating the David Ortiz Children’s Fund to support needy children in the Dominican Republic and New England. David’s impact on the Red Sox and the city of Boston is widely recognized, and considered one of the greatest players in franchise history.
