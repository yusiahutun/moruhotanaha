Brené Brown Biography TimeLine

Brené Brown is a research professor, speaker, and author known for her work on vulnerability, shame, and empathy. Here is a timeline of Brené Brown’s life events:

  • 1965: Brené Brown was born in San Antonio, Texas.
  • 1995: Brown earned her Bachelor’s in Social Work from the University of Texas at Austin.
  • 1996: Brown earned her Master’s in Social Work from the University of Houston.
  • 2002: Brown earned her PhD in Social Work from the University of Houston.
  • 2007: Brown’s first book, “I Thought It Was Just Me (But It Isn’t): Making the Journey from ‘What Will People Think?’ to ‘I Am Enough,'” was published.
  • 2010: Brown’s TEDxHouston talk, “The Power of Vulnerability,” went viral, gaining millions of views.
  • 2012: Brown’s book, “Daring Greatly: How the Courage to Be Vulnerable Transforms the Way We Live, Love, Parent, and Lead,” was published.
  • 2013: Brown was featured on Oprah Winfrey’s SuperSoul Sunday program.
  • 2015: Brown’s book, “Rising Strong,” was published, focusing on getting back up after failure or disappointment.
  • 2017: Brown’s Netflix special, “Brené Brown: The Call to Courage,” premiered, sharing her research on vulnerability and courage with a broader audience.
  • 2019: Brown’s book, “Dare to Lead: Brave Work. Tough Conversations. Whole Hearts,” was published, focusing on how to lead with empathy and vulnerability.
  • 2020: Brown launched her podcast, “Unlocking Us,” where she has conversations with thought leaders and explores topics related to vulnerability, courage, and empathy.

Brené Brown has inspired millions to embrace vulnerability and live more authentic, wholehearted lives through her research, writing, and speaking engagements. Her work has been recognized by numerous awards and accolades, including a spot on Time magazine’s list of 100 most influential people worldwide.
