O.J. Mayo was born on November 5, 1987.

O.J. Mayo, whose full name is Ovinton J’Anthony Mayo, was born on November 5, 1987, in Huntington, West Virginia.

He was a high school basketball prodigy.

From a young age, Mayo showcased incredible talent on the basketball court, earning him recognition as one of the top high school players in the country.

Mayo was named the Ohio Mr. Basketball in 2006.

During his senior year at North College Hill High School in Cincinnati, Ohio, Mayo received the prestigious Ohio Mr. Basketball award.

He played college basketball at USC.

After graduating from high school, Mayo played one season at the University of Southern California (USC) before declaring for the NBA Draft.

Mayo was the third overall pick in the 2008 NBA Draft.

His exceptional talent and potential led to him being selected as the third overall pick by the Minnesota Timberwolves in the 2008 NBA Draft.

He was traded to the Memphis Grizzlies on draft night.

Shortly after being drafted by the Timberwolves, Mayo was traded to the Memphis Grizzlies in exchange for Kevin Love.

O.J. Mayo won the NBA Rookie of the Month award in January 2009.

During his first season in the NBA, Mayo made an immediate impact and was recognized for his outstanding performance by receiving the NBA Rookie of the Month award in January 2009.

He had a successful NBA career.

Throughout his NBA career, Mayo played for multiple teams including the Memphis Grizzlies, Dallas Mavericks, and Milwaukee Bucks. He achieved various milestones and contributed significantly to each team he played for.

Mayo was suspended for violating the NBA’s anti-drug policy.

In 2016, Mayo faced a setback in his career when he received a two-year suspension for violating the NBA’s anti-drug policy.

He played overseas during his suspension.

While serving his suspension, Mayo continued his basketball career by playing overseas in leagues such as the Chinese Basketball Association.

O.J. Mayo attempted an NBA comeback in 2019.

After completing his suspension, Mayo expressed his desire to return to the NBA and made attempts to revive his professional career.

He is known for his scoring ability.

Throughout his career, Mayo established himself as a skilled scorer, showcasing his ability to shoot from long range and create opportunities for his team.

Mayo has represented the United States in international competitions.

With his talent and skills, Mayo had the honor of representing the United States in international basketball competitions such as the FIBA World Championship.

He officially retired from professional basketball in 2020.

In December 2020, Mayo announced his retirement from professional basketball, concluding a successful career that spanned over a decade.
