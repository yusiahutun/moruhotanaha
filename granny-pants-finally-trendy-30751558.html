Granny pants are finally trendy with models going wild for big knickers

 Posted on March 7, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  366 words  |  Aldo Pusey

We'll be raiding granny's knicker draw if recent catwalk shows and celebrity sightings are anything to go by.

Big pants were shown on models at the Rotate show at Copenhagen Fashion Week ahead of summer next year – all big frilly knickers and large bejewelled panties.

“I never knew I needed such jazzy underwear,” said one fan of the partywear designers on Instagram.

The Copenhagen-based brand showed the knickers du jour on the catwalks teamed with matchy matchy minuscule sequin bombers and, in contrast with a further style, a big black leather trenchcoat or a huge feather sombrero.

Granny pants aren't just for brave fashionistas or catwalk models either.

Earlier this year actress Drew Barrymore confessed she's a fan of granny's knickers on her podcast, The Drew Barrymore Show.

Barrymore, the E.T. child star, discussed how models have been wearing granny panties and also revealed why she's a fan of that particularly roomy style of knickers.

She told her followers: "Basically, you know their entire butt's covered. You got nothing going up the crack. That's why I wear them."

Victoria Beckham included some big, beige, knitted knickers in her upcoming summer 2024 collection, too.

But not everyone appreciates the comfortable knickers from gran's closet though.

When Posh Spice styled-up the undies with a green bag and cream, over-the-knee stockings, one Instagrammer asked the celebrity fashion designer: “Is this a joke?”

It's certainly a brave look for the summer wardrobes of next year.

You can wear yours as standard comfy underwear a la Drew Barrymore, or with stockings like the Victoria Beckham variety.

Or with tiny vests and huge, cream feather sombreros like the Rotate designers of Copenhagen where there seemed to be more fabric to the hats than the bottom halves.

One thing's for sure – the granny knickers trend will work for WFH babes on a Zoom call!
