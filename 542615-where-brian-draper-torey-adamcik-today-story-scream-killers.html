Who are Brian Draper and Torey Adamcik?

Brian Draper and Torey Adamcik were the murder perpetrators of Cassie Jo Stoddard. Brian Lee Draper was born on 21 March 1990 and had spent much of his childhood in Utah but moved with the family to Pocatello, Idaho, United States, when he was a teenager.

On the other hand, Torey Michael Adamcik was born on 14 June 1990. Brian met Torey Adamcik while both were students at Pocatello High School, where Cassie Stoddart was attending. She, along with Draper and Adamcik, were 11th graders at the prestigious school.

Brian Draper and Torey Adamcik commit murder

On 22 September 2006, Cassie Jo Stoddart, a 16-year-old high school student, was murdered by her classmates Brian Lee Draper and Torey Michael. She was house-sitting for her aunt Allison and uncle Frank in Bannock County, Idaho, United States, at the time.

The victim had invited her boyfriend, Matt Beckham, to join her at the house. Beckham, in turn, invited his close friend Torey Adamcik, who came along with Brian Draper. After spending the evening together, Brian and Adamcik left but unlocked the basement door beforehand so they could return later that night to carry out their plan.

A few hours later, they returned wearing dark clothes, white masks, and gloves. They crept into the property through the basement door and unsuccessfully tried to lure Beckham and Cassie downstairs by making loud noises.

Noticing his girlfriend's uneasiness, Matt called his mother, asking if he could spend the night with Cassie to ease her mind. His mother denied the request but offered Stoddard to come to their home for the night and drop her off early the following day.

Stoddard felt obligated to stay at the house and declined the offer. Matt Beckham was later picked up by his mother at around 10.30 pm. From the basement, Adamcik and Draper heard Matt leave. They went upstairs and stabbed Cassie about 30 times.

Dr Charles Garrison, a forensic pathologist, later testified that most of the wounds struck Stoddart's heart, particularly her right ventricle. Her killers, Brian Lee Draper and Torey Adamcik, claimed that they were inspired to kill Stoddart by the slasher film Scream, which led to them being nicknamed "The Scream Killers".

How did Brian Draper and Torey get caught?

On 27 September 2006, Brian and Torey were arrested and interviewed by police officers. This was after Matt Beckham informed authorities that they were the last people to see Stoddart alive.

During the interrogations, they blamed each other. Draper claimed he was in the same room with Adamcik when Cassey Jo Stoddart was murdered but denied killing her. Later, he admitted to stabbing her under commands from Adamcik.

Draper eventually led detectives to Black Rock Canyon, where they had disposed of their clothes, cameras, and murder weapons. They had attempted to burn the videotapes of their horrific confession, but fortunately, detectives recovered the footage and used it in their investigations.

Brian Draper and Torey Adamcik sentence

What happened to Brian Draper and Torey Adamcik? Brian and Torey were charged with first-degree murder and conspiracy to commit first-degree murder. Despite their young age at the time, they were tried as adults.

During the trial, the prosecution disclosed that Draper claimed Eric Harris and Dylan Klebold inspired him. Eric and Dylan were high school students who executed the Columbine High School massacre on 20 April 1999. On the other hand, Adamcik was inspired by the Scream horror film.

Brian was convicted on 17 April 2007, while his counterpart Adamcik was convicted on 8 June 2007. They received a mandatory life sentence for the murder and thirty years to life for conspiracy to murder on 21 August 2007.

Brian Draper and Torey Adamcik documentary

Adamcik and Draper recorded documentary-style videos about how they were horror movie fans, especially Scream, and wanted to carry out a similar murder in real life. The documentary would document their attempt to enact the masked Scream killer by picking on their classmates one by one. They started a "Death List" of other potential victims, and Stoddart was at the top of it.

Where are Brian Draper and Torey Adamcik today?

Brian Draper and Torey Adamcik are serving their life sentence in prison. They were first sent to Idaho State Correctional Institution. Adamcik was later moved to Saguaro Correctional Center in Arizona, while Draper remained in Idaho.
