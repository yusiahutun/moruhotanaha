James Arthur was born on March 2, 1988.

James Arthur, the British singer-songwriter, was born on March 2, 1988, in Middlesbrough, England. His birth date makes him a Pisces.

He rose to fame after winning the ninth season of The X Factor UK.

James Arthur gained widespread recognition in 2012 when he emerged as the winner of The X Factor UK. His soulful voice and unique style captivated both the judges and the audience.

His debut single, “Impossible,” became a massive hit.

“Impossible,” James Arthur’s debut single, released shortly after winning The X Factor, reached number one on the UK Singles Chart. The heartfelt song resonated with listeners and showcased his powerful vocal range.

James Arthur has released four studio albums.

Since his win on The X Factor, James Arthur has released four studio albums, including his self-titled debut album and “Back from the Edge,” which topped the UK Albums Chart.

He has collaborated with various artists throughout his career.

James Arthur has collaborated with several renowned musicians, including Anne-Marie on the hit single “Rewrite the Stars” and Emeli Sandé on the heartfelt song “Roses.”

He has overcome mental health challenges.

James Arthur won the 2017 Teen Choice Award for Choice Music: R&B/Hip-Hop Song.
