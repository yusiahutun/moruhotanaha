An essential ingredient of any science fiction show is at least one cast member capable of becoming a nerd sex symbol.

And there is little doubt forthcoming television show Revolution has found a winning formula by casting Tracy Spiridakos in a lead role.

The actress looks positively ravishing in a semi nude pose for GQ, which comes in the run up to the show's debut in September.

Winning formula: Revolution star Tracy Spiridakos is sure to have many new fans thanks to this GQ photoshoot

And she is even more certain to win hearts after confessing to the magazine that she is actually quite the geek herself.

Tracy, who plays Charlie Matheson on the show, confessed she is addicted to computer games, and that she likes nothing more after a hard day on set than going on a virtual killing spree.

She said: 'I'm obsessed with my PlayStation. "I'll come home and plug away at Fallout for a couple of hours.

'Or if I'm feeling the hacking and slashing, I'll play a little God of War.'

Leading the way: Science fiction nerds are sure to go ga ga for Tracy in Revolution

Revolution is set in a post-apocalyptic future, fifteen years after an unknown phenomenon permanently disabled all advanced technology on the planet.

Everything from computers and electronics to car engines and batteries have been affected.

People were forced to adapt to a world without technology, and due to the collapse of public order, many areas are ruled by warlords and militias.

The series focuses on the Matheson family, who possess an item that is the key to not only finding out what happened fifteen years ago, but also a possible way to reverse the effects.

The show has an impressive pedigree, for as well as being produced by Star Trek director JJ Abrams, Iron Man director Jon Favreau helmed the pilot episode.
