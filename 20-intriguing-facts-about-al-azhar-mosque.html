A Historic Gem

The Al-Azhar Mosque, located in Cairo, Egypt, is one of the oldest functioning Islamic institutions in the world.

Symbol of Knowledge

Al-Azhar Mosque is not only a place of worship but also a renowned center for Islamic learning and education.

Majestic Architecture

The mosque showcases stunning architectural elements, with its impressive minarets and domes that dominate the Cairo skyline.

It Was Built in 970 AD

The construction of Al-Azhar Mosque began in 970 AD during the rule of the Fatimid caliph, Al-Mu’izz li-Din Allah.

Beautiful Calligraphy

The mosque is adorned with intricate calligraphy, showcasing the beauty of Arabic script.

The Main Prayer Hall

The main prayer hall of Al-Azhar Mosque can accommodate up to 20,000 worshippers at a time.

The Imam’s Minbar

The mosque features an intricately carved wooden pulpit known as the Imam’s minbar, from which sermons are delivered.

The Al-Azhar University

Adjacent to the mosque is the prestigious Al-Azhar University, one of the world’s oldest universities.

Landmark of Tolerance

Al-Azhar Mosque has played a vital role in promoting interfaith dialogue and religious tolerance.

A Hub for Islamic Studies

The Al-Azhar Mosque and University have produced countless Islamic scholars and intellectuals over the centuries.

The Beautiful Courtyard

The mosque is characterized by its large courtyard surrounded by colonnades and adorned with lush greenery.

Significant Restorations

Throughout its long history, the Al-Azhar Mosque has undergone several renovations and restoration projects.

An Architectural Influence

The mosque’s architectural style has influenced other Islamic structures, both regionally and globally.

Cultural Events and Exhibitions

The Al-Azhar Mosque complex hosts various cultural events and exhibitions, showcasing Islamic arts and heritage.

UNESCO World Heritage Site

In 1979, Al-Azhar Mosque was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognizing its cultural and historical significance.

A Gathering Place

The mosque’s courtyard is a popular gathering place for locals and visitors, providing a tranquil and serene atmosphere.

The Grand Entrance

The main entrance to the mosque is adorned with beautifully carved wooden doors and intricate Islamic geometric patterns.

Al-Azhar Park

Adjacent to the mosque is Al-Azhar Park, a peaceful oasis that offers stunning views of the Cairo cityscape.

A Global Symbol of Islamic Culture

Al-Azhar Mosque stands as a symbol of the rich Islamic culture and heritage, attracting tourists from all over the world.

Continuity and Modernity

While preserving its rich history, Al-Azhar Mosque has embraced modern technology and initiatives to cater to the needs of the contemporary Muslim community.

With its rich history, architectural grandeur, and significant role in Islamic education, Al-Azhar Mosque remains a captivating landmark that showcases the beauty of Islamic culture and knowledge.

FAQs

1. When was Al-Azhar Mosque built?

Al-Azhar Mosque was built in the year 970 AD, during the reign of the Fatimid Caliphate dynasty.

2. Is Al-Azhar Mosque open to non-Muslim visitors?

Yes, Al-Azhar Mosque is open to non-Muslim visitors. However, visitors are required to respect the rules and customs of the mosque, such as dressing modestly and removing their shoes before entering.

3. Are there any guided tours available?

Yes, guided tours are available at Al-Azhar Mosque. These tours offer a deeper insight into the mosque’s history, architecture, and significance. It is recommended to book a guided tour in advance to ensure availability.

4. Can women visit Al-Azhar Mosque?

Yes, women are welcome to visit Al-Azhar Mosque. However, they are advised to dress modestly and cover their heads as a sign of respect for the religious customs.

5. Is photography allowed inside the mosque?

Photography is not allowed inside the mosque, but you can take pictures of the exterior and courtyard areas.

6. Are there any entrance fees?

No, there are no entrance fees to visit Al-Azhar Mosque. However, donations are appreciated to support the maintenance and preservation of the mosque.

7. Is there a dress code for visiting Al-Azhar Mosque?

Yes, there is a dress code for visiting Al-Azhar Mosque. Both men and women are required to dress modestly, covering their shoulders and knees. Women are also required to cover their heads.
