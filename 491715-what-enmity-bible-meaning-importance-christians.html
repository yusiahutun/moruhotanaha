What is enmity in the Bible? Meaning and importance to Christians

 Posted on March 13, 2024  |  6 minutes  |  1274 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

For thousands of years, the Bible has strongly influenced everyday language and culture among believers. This impacts society and the behaviour of Christians on different occasions. While the meaning of some phrases like enmity doesn’t necessarily align with its initial context, it has a significant impact on Christians. So, what is enmity in the Bible?

Enmity in the Bible is derived from a Latin word, inimicus, which means unfriend. Even though the word comes from Latin, the concept runs across various verses and instances throughout the Bible. As a result, it carries a deep meaning and great importance to Christians.

Enmity in the Bible

Enemity or enmity is deep-rooted hatred, strife or inherent opposition between two entities. What does enmity in Bible mean? Enmity means different things since it has been used in various situations throughout the Bible. Some of these instances include:

The Garden of Eden

What is enmity in Genesis? In the garden of Eden in the book of Genesis, enmity is seen when God declares condemnation upon the serpent. According to the scriptures, a woman’s hatred for snakes was caused by a spiritual reason. This represents the intense enmity between the woman and the serpent.

I will put enmity between you and the woman, and between your offspring and hers - Genesis 3:15

Love for the world

In the book of James, the Lord states that friendship with the world equates to enmity with God. This means that all the corrupt and undesirable ways of the earth defy God's holiness. This is also emphasized in a couple of other verses, including:

The mind governed by the flesh is hostile to God. – Romans 8:7Do not love the world or anything in the world. If anyone loves the world, love for the Father is not in them. – 1st John 2:15

Importance to Christians

What is the importance of understanding enmity in the Bible for Christians? First, knowledge is handy in enabling Christians to know how it manifests and the benefits of avoiding worldly pleasures and focusing on God.

Enmity revelation

There are various effects of enmity on Christians as a consequence and cohort of sin. In these ways, it is revealed to human beings in the following areas:

1. Person vs God

The first problem happens when people become enemies with God. This is primarily caused by human beings’ selfish choices and thoughts that distance them from their creator.

2. Person vs himself

Enmity with God means that one is going against the purpose and design of God. This comes with several consequences on them, including:

  • Hypocrisy
  • Inner struggles
  • Anxiety
  • Lack of purpose

Enmity causes conflict with other people. For instance, the Bible highlights numerous cases of conflict among people due to enmity. They include:

  • Fight for power between Adam and Eve in the garden of Eden
  • Cain killed his brother Abel
  • People enslaving and killings others over resources and land in the Old Testament

4. Person vs environment

Enmity causes conflict between people and the environment they dwell in. In the garden of Eden, Adam and Eve’s sin led to a lack of abundance on the land. Adam had to till the land and produce his food from the sweat of his brow.

While enmity causes conflict, fear, destruction and eventually death, God offers a solution to all the believers, as seen in the various Bible verses:

1. Peace with God

By repenting and submitting to God, he restores believers to reality where he controls their lives.
