13 Cute Jean Skirt Outfits to Wear Year-Round

 Posted on February 10, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  753 words  |  Martina Birk

Jean skirts are universally flattering. Whether your go-to is an itty bitty mini or you're partial to a below-the-knee hemline, there are myriad ways to wear a denim skirt. Celebs and the fashion set are stepping out in maxi and midi varieties and sporting looks that prove you don't need to show a ton of skin to have a sultry denim skirt moment.

However, if you do reach for a shorter length, you can still achieve sophistication by incorporating knee-high boots or an oversized blazer. It's all about balance and keeping proportions in check. Regardless of your personal taste, we guarantee you'll fall in love with one of these jean skirt outfit ideas.

Ahead, we've rounded up 13 ways to wear this trendy and timeless wardrobe staple according to street style stars.

With Statement-Making Ruffles

Toss out any ideas you once had about long denim skirts being outdated. Pairing one with a cropped statement top delivers major main character energy, even—and especially—when worn with white sneakers.

With a Denim Corset

We've said it once, and we'll say it again, Canadian tuxedos will never truly go out of style. This season's trendiest take on this timeless trend combines the snatched fit of a corset top with the lengthening effect of a denim midi skirt. To really drive the point home, add a denim blazer over the top.

With a Sunny Sweater

Banish fall and winter blues with a brightly colored sweater and matching heels. We love a mood-boosting canary yellow, but you should go with whatever hue speaks to you—and flatters your skin tone, too.

With a Crochet Top

Crocheted tops add a bit of warm weather whimsy to a fall 'fit, and when paired with an asymmetrical denim skirt and colorful accessories, create a playful but effortless aesthetic.

With an Oversized Blazer

We are obsessed with oversized blazers this year. If pairing with a maxiskirt, consider a fitted top or go after the bracket look to keep proportions in check.

With Normcore Separates

This denim skirt outfit idea is all about adding something a little extra to basics you already have on hand. Crew socks, combined with chunky loafers and a belt, elevates a denim skirt, and an asymmetrical neckline breathes new life into an old white tank.

With a Matching Headband

A denim mini and graphic tee are a classic combo that will never go out of style. However, to take this look to the next level, consider a printed variety of the skirt and a similarly patterned headband. There's no need for the denim skirt and headband to match precisely, but you'll want to choose similar shades of blue to bring the look together.

With Some Edge

This is not your elementary school art teacher's long jean skirt outfit. Give any denim maxi a modern update by adding a sheer top and your favorite leather jacket. Complete the look with knee-high boots and color-coordinated accessories.

With a Vintage Feel

A midi or maxi denim skirt will help you land the '70s glam aesthetic that's having a revival. Adding a cropped sequined top and cardigan combo plus a pair of T-strap heels creates a vintage vibe that's sultry but not costume-y.

With Basics

There's nothing more classic than a denim miniskirt, a plain white tank or T-shirt, and a pair of crisp white sneakers. No need to fuss with this formula for a fun and flirtatious look, but consider adding a denim jacket as an extra layer or a stylish accessory à la Barbara Palvin.

With Cowboy Boots

To elevate a basic denim miniskirt, we recommend experimenting with footwear. Cowboy boots add instant personality to any outfit. When paired with tights, style them just above your waistline for a Bella Hadid-approved look.

With a Graphic Tee

To cultivate a stylish but casual off-duty model vibe, pair your favorite denim miniskirt with a pair of statement sneakers and a graphic tee. It's visually and literally effortless.

With a Pop of Color

One way to work a bit of color into a jean skirt 'fit is to skip overthinking or over-styling; instead, simply grab a denim skirt in a colorful wash like this trending shade of green chartreuse.
