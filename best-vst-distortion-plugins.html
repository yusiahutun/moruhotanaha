The 7 Best VST Distortion Plugins for Your Audio

 Posted on February 19, 2024  |  6 minutes  |  1262 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Audio distortion plugins can make all the difference in making diverse sounds come alive. They can add depth, character, contrast, and even entirely new, weird, and wonderful sounds.

The right distortion plugin can fundamentally raise the quality of your audio projects. We'll go over the best free and paid distortion plugins, so you can find the best fit for you.

The Best Free VST Distortion Plugins

If you're just getting started in the world of audio production, remember that there are some quality free distortion plugins that might just meet your needs. If you're a Logic Pro user, you may want to check out how to use each of Logic's distortion plugins.

1. CamelCrusher

CamelCrusher is a renowned free distortion plugin known for its versatility and easy-to-use interface. This makes it an excellent choice for both beginners and experienced producers. Whether you want to add subtle warmth to your vocals or create a wall of aggressive guitar distortion, CamelCrusher can deliver this and more.

CamelCrusher offers a variety of presets (or patches) and four modules to tweak your sound: a distortion section; a compressor section; a master output section; and a filter section. Look into how to use EQs and filters to help you out in this area. Whatever your preferred genre or style, the versatility of CamelCrusher makes it a tool you'll want to try out.

2. Ruina

Ruina is a distortion plugin that differs from the norm. Instead of emulating different types of hardware-related distortion, it focuses on pushing the boundaries regarding sonic experimentation and destruction.

It offers many presets, seven different types of distortion, and even a DOOM mode for apocalyptic audio manipulation. From lush timbres to tonal destruction, Ruina can work well on vocals, drums, guitars, and more.

Ruina provides a unique combination of tools including phase shifting, multiband saturation, wavefolding, and filters. This allows you to create wild and chaotic textures that can add unpredictable elements to your audio. If you're a fan of sound design and sonic chaos, Ruina is worth your time.

3. Saturation Knob

Saturation Knob is the simplest plugin on this list—one knob to control the degree of saturation—but by no means does this take away from its quality. It represents a great way to add some extra warmth and harmonics to your audio.

Saturation Knob emulates the behavior of analog tape saturation and this is precisely what makes it special. Often, both live and digital recordings can sound hollow or bland. Effective use of this plugin can provide that elusive analog warmth most audio producers aim for.

When used subtly, it can make your tracks sound more organic and lively. Intensive usage leads to a tape saturation crunch that's perfect for adding excitement to vocals, bass parts, and percussion. For a touch of analog color and a mix of simplicity and efficiency, you need look no further than the Saturation Knob plugin.

The Best Paid VST Distortion Plugins

If you're looking into the best paid distortion plugins, remember to check when the developers put their plugins on sale. Time your purchase well, and you can save a lot of money.

4. Decapitator

Decapitator by Soundtoys is widely considered one of the best analog saturation and distortion plugins available in the market. Like with other Soundtoys plugins, Decapitator demonstrates incredible attention to detail in emulating the nuances of analog gear. The subtle nonlinear nature of analog distortion and its harmonic richness are meticulously captured by this plugin.

Decapitator provides five different saturation models, each inspired by iconic analog units. Whether you're aiming for the smooth saturation of a tape machine or the gritty edge of a vintage console, Decapitator has you covered.

The Drive and Tone dials allow precise shaping of the distortion, while the Punish button takes things to the next level for intensive saturation. Blend the dry signal with the distorted signal with the Mix knob, and you can find the right balance for your audio.

For a wide array of cutting-edge analog distortion flavors within a highly versatile tool, add Decapitator to your creative toolset.

5. Trash 2

Trash 2 is a powerful distortion and audio manipulation plugin that excels in the realms of sound design and experimentation. The Preset button in the bottom-right draws up its extensive list of presets categorized into distinct styles. You can then tweak your sound by selecting one of the seven types of distortion in the Trash module and the other modules along the bottom of the interface.

These modules include a Convolve module with 100 impulse responses, two filter modules, a Dynamics module, and a Delay module. Its intuitive design lets you alter the signal chain for in-built effects by selecting the Graph option. This gives you an even greater degree of control over your sound.

Once you've got to grips with some of the controls, the sonic possibilities Trash 2 offers are endless. You can generate entirely original sounds that can range from the melodic to the demonic. The presets often provide a great base sound to build from, and a simple piano or vocal part can morph into something completely different.

While iZotope has discontinued its support for Trash 2, you can still find it via other online markets. If you're after original ways to morph existing sounds and create new ones, Trash 2 is the plugin for you.

6. Saturn 2

Saturn 2 by Fabfilter is known for its pristine sound quality, excellent visual design (as Fabfilter always delivers), and versatility. It's designed to provide precise control over diverse forms of harmonic manipulation and distortion across multiple frequency bands.

Saturn 2 offers a variety of distortion styles based on classic analog gear as well as five creative FX distortion modes to intensively alter your audio. From warm tape saturation to aggressive digital clipping, you can input the perfect amount of distortion to suit your desired style. The plugin also provides extensive modulation options to sculpt the characteristics of your sound.

The transparent and precise sounds Saturn 2 provides make it an excellent tool for pushing the quality of your audio into professional realms. It can work great on all kinds of individual tracks, and can also serve as the final sparkle on an entire mix.

7. Harmonics

Harmonics specializes in its emulation of the harmonic characteristics of analog tape and tube saturation; its visual design similarly matches such hardware gear. It offers five component-modeled distortion modes, cutting-edge dynamic transient control, and a slew of high-quality presets.

Given its focus as an analog distortion emulator, you'll be hard-pressed to find a better plugin that captures the essence of tape and tube distortion. The distortion Harmonics offers can work wonders on your audio, adding color, excitement, and vibrancy. Add it to your vocals or guitars for a vintage feel, or to your digital instruments for a more organic touch.

Include Harmonics in your digital toolkit to integrate the very best of the lush sounds analog gear provides as well as their biting edge.

Get the Best Distortion for Your Audio

The manner in which distortion plugins enrich and enliven diverse audio makes them vital tools during production. If you're on a budget, try out the versatile CamelCrusher distortion tool or Ruina for its chaotic and experimental audio manipulation. For simplicity and harmonic distortion, use the Saturation Knob.

For the cutting-edge in the distortion field, go for Decapitator or Harmonics. For mangled and original sounds try out Trash 2, and use Saturn 2 for its extensive sonic possibilities and precision. Do so, and your audio will take a substantial leap in quality.
