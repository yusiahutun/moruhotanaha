Contrary to popular belief, Devon Allen is not yet married. He rather seems to be dating Samantha Bowe.

Devon previously dated Australian track and field athlete Morgan Mitchell, but it seems like they are no longer together. 

On January 6, 2023, he shared a photo of himself with Samantha captioning it with a heart emoji. Samantha left a comment, ”Lover” on the post. 

From the post, it seems like Allen is in a romantic relationship with her.

Devon Allen is a wide receiver for the Philadelphia Eagles of the NFL. Allen signed a three-year undrafted rookie deal with the Eagles on April 8, 2022. He was later waived on August 30, 2022, but quickly signed to the practice squad the following day.

Allen is also a track and field athlete who specializes in the 110-meter hurdles. He is a three-time U.S. national champion in the 110-meter hurdles (2014, 2016, and 2018).

Devon is also a two-time Olympian, reaching the finals in the 2016 Rio de Janeiro and 2020 Tokyo Olympics. It is interesting to note that his personal best time of 12.84 seconds, set in 2022, ranks third in history.

Who Is Devon Allen Wife? Meet Girlfriend Samantha Bowe

She then pursued a Master’s degree in Sports Law and Business at Arizona State University.

Samantha attended the University of New Mexico, where she achieved notable distinctions, such as being placed on the Dean’s list and receiving the “Scholar Athlete” award.

Following her graduation, she worked as a Performance Specialist at Life Time Inc. before joining Axon as an Account Executive in September 2022.

Additionally, Samantha has entrepreneurial experience as the former owner of Drip By Sam LLC. Currently, she is passionately advocating for increased women’s participation and empowerment in the Tech sector.

She says that she wants to make the world a safer place by changing the way law enforcement uses technology.

Devon Allen Was Disqualified In 2022. Was He Too Fast For The Sensors?

In the July of 2022, Allen was disqualified from the men’s 110-meter hurdles final for false starting.

The starts which are faster than 0.1 seconds are considered excessive. Allen’s reaction before the start of the race was 0.099 seconds which caused his removal from the race.

Allen stated after the race that he was certain he hadn’t reacted until he heard the gun. He went on to say that being one-thousandth of a second too fast was disheartening.

The runner admitted his own quickness and expressed that he was feeling frustrated by the scenario. It was not only him, but the 13,000-person crowd at Hayward Field was stunned too.

Allen qualified in the heats in 13.47 seconds and the semis in 13.09 seconds. The runner expected to take a medal, if not win, in that race.
