Family and friends remember victims killed in Lafayette theater shooting

 Posted on January 31, 2024  |  6 minutes  |  1184 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Emotional family members remembered the victims shot dead at a Louisiana movie theater during funeral services on Monday.

The loved ones of Mayci Breaux, 21, and Jillian Johnson, 33, attended services held at Church of the Assumption in Franklin, Louisiana and the Delhomme Funeral Home in Lafayette on Monday.

Breaux and Johnson were killed by an Alabama drifter who opened fire inside a crowded movie theater in Lafayette last Thursday, in the latest act of random gun violence to shock the United States.

'Mayci was a loving, kind, beautiful, and vibrant young lady, who had a wonderful personality and a smile that would light up even the darkest of rooms,' her obituary read.

She was from Franklin, Louisiana where she attended Hanson Memorial High School and was voted 'Most Beautiful' by her 2012 graduating class and was co-captain of the Varsity cheerleading team.

Her family said she was living in Lafayette at the time with her long-term boyfriend Matthew Rodriguez - who was also shot in the chest, but not critically injured. 

Their home in Lafayette was not far from Louisiana State University's Eunice campus, where Breaux was studying to be a ultrasound and radiology technician. 

She was expected to graduate in 2017 and was looking forward to a future with Rodriguez.

Johnson, a musician and owner of a Lafayette gift shop, leaves behind her stepdaughter and husband of two years, Jason Brown, with whom she ran a local gift shop called the Red Arrow Workshop.

The couple also owned a clothing store called Parish Ink which had two locations in Lafayette and one in New Orleans.

Friends of Johnson, like Pudd Sharp, remembered her as a woman of talent and warmth. 

At an event to remember the victims he pointed out people wearing T-shirts she designed and described her as a funny, talented and welcoming woman. 

He said Johnson was a 'tastemaker,' the type of person that if you showed up at a club or restaurant or an event and she was there, you knew you were in the right place. 

In a post on Red Arrow Workshop's Facebook page it said Johnson made a name for herself as one of South Louisiana's most gifted and beloved creative professionals.

'Jillian Johnson, who made a name for herself as one of South Louisiana’s most gifted and beloved creative professionals, and who was at the forefront of the region’s cultural and artistic renaissance, passed away on Thursday, July 23, 2015,' the post read.

'She leaves behind a legacy of beautiful artwork and design, brilliant and meaningful entrepreneurship, civic advocacy, and timeless song. For all of those who knew and loved her, she was the model of a life well lived.' 

Mourners gathered to pay tribute to the victims of the Lafayette theater shooting during a memorial candlelight vigil and prayer service at a church in Lafayette, Louisiana, on Sunday night.

Posters of the victims were displayed during the service at the Cathedral of St John the Evangelist.

Police say John Russell Houser, 59, stood up about 20 minutes into the showing of Trainwreck at The Grand 16 movie theater and began firing, injuring nine others, before ultimately taking his own life.

Questions continue to surround Houser's motive for the shooting, his background and his mental state.
