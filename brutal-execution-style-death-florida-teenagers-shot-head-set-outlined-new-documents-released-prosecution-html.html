Brutal details of the deaths of Winter Park High School students Nicholas Presha and Jeremy Stewart were released today by investigators in Orange County, Florida.

Shot 'execution style', Stewart, 18, died from a single gunshot to the head while Presha, 16,  was shot once in the back of the head and twice more in the face before both were set alight along the Cady Way Trail in April.

The new evidence pieces together the pair's final few hours and is part of the the case against Jesse Davis and Hector Rodriguez who are charged with two counts of first-degree murder and two counts of armed kidnapping.

Records: Nicholas Presha, (left), and Jeremy Stewart, (right), were shot in the head and set on fire and left along the Cady Way Trail on April 15

Interviewing dozens of friends of the victims and the accused, authorities have compiled a 200 page report which suggests the teenagers ill-fated evening began when Presha and Stewart sneaked out of their homes and met up at a Race Trac gas station on Aloma Avenue in Orange County.

Around midnight, the two asked Rodriguez to buy them beer and surveillance images from the station show Rodriguez purchasing alcohol around 12.48 a.m on the morning of April 15 according to the Orlando Sentinel.

The two best friends were known for asking homeless people to buy them beer along the Cady Way Trail and also for 'car hopping', which is pulling open car door handles to see if the cars are locked and then stealing the contents of the unlocked vehicle.

Investigators also discovered from friends that Presha and Stewart had managed to steal guns during one of their 'car hopping' evenings prior to the events of April 15.

Behind bars: Hector Manuel Rodriguez, left, and Jesse Brandon Davis have both been arrested and charged with the murders of the two Florida teens

The two teens and Rodriguez went to a Steak 'n Shake restaurant at around 2 a.m. where they were joined by the other suspect Jesse Davis.

Rodgriguez has told investigators that the boys were trying to sell the pair stolen guns and that Presha had a weapon with him.

Returning to Stewart's home so that he could pick up his stolen .40 calibre Glock handgun, the four traveled on to Davis' mother's home on Orange and Aloma.

While inside the house, Davis is reported by witnesses to have asked to see the Glock and when handed the weapon, he turned it on the teenagers telling them to 'Get the f**k down!'.

The home of Jesse Davis where the two teenagers realised that they were being double crossed as they attempted to sell stolen guns to Davis and Rodriguez

Appearing in court after their arrest in April Jesse David (left) and Hector Rodriguez are currently in Orange County Jail having been denied bail

Witnesses in the Davis household said that Davis fired a warning shot and then hit Stewart in the face with the but of the weapon and then ordered the teens to remove their clothes.

Davis' 42-year-old girflriend told investigators that she saw the pair lying face down naked on the floor and that both teens hands were tied behind their backs.

Investigators believe that Rodriguez and Davis loaded the teenagers into the back of Davis' car and drove to another gas station near to the Cady Way Trail where they are seen to act suspiciously by surveillance cameras with the trunk of the car open.

The gruesome scene where the two boys were found by cyclists just 20 minutes after being set alight on Cady Way in Orange County, Florida

Around 5.48 a.m. it is alleged that the teenagers were killed and set alight where they were found by cyclists before 6.10 a.m.

Davis' lawyer has told the court that his client is incompetent due to a history of mental illness, however any evidence of a cover-up as outlined in the surveillance footage would compromise that claim.

Since their arrests April Jesse Brandon Davis and Hector Manuel Rodriguez  have remained in Orange County Jail denied bail by a judge.

Both men have been charged with first-degree murder and kidnapping in the deaths of 16-year-old Nicholas Presha and 18-year-old Jeremy Stewart in April, who were found badly burned on a popular biking trail in Winter Park, Florida.

Tragic: The bodies of Nicholas Presha, 16, left, and Jeremy Stewart, 18, right, were found still burning on April 15 on Florida's Cady Way Trail

Orange County Sheriff Jerry Demings said the felons were both ‘evil and dangerous criminals.’ He says that the teens were likely killed as they were trying to sell the men handguns.

The Orange County Sheriff's Office reported in April that the bodies found near the popular Cady Way Trail were identified by the medical examiner as two Winter Park High School students, Presha and Stewart.

In a 911 call released today, one of the cyclists who discovered the teenagers tells a dispatcher: 'Um, I can't tell if this is two mannequins or two people burning.'

Scene: The bodies were discovered by two cyclists along the popular Cady Way Trail in Winter Park, Florida

Gruesome: Investigators rushed to the scene after receiving a 911 from a cyclist who didn't know if the bodies burning were mannequins or people

A source close to the investigation told WFTV the teenagers were shot in the head execution-style and that the killing was somehow related to a gun deal.

The students had been in trouble with the law before. Police records show Presha and Stewart were arrested in January on charges of grand theft of a motor vehicle and resisting arrest without violence. Both were charged as juveniles.

According to the State Attorney's Office, they entered a Teen Court program allowing minors to avoid prosecution if they stay out of trouble. 

The incident has meanwhile shaken students at Winter Park High, where the teenagers were juniors.

Junior Alexa Hussein called the incident 'shocking' and 'hard to process' in an interview with the Sentinel.

'It's not just some random person. It's someone one who you know and hung out with,' she said.

Shaken: Students at Winter Park High, where the students were juniors, called the incident 'shocking'

School officials said grief counselors are on hand at the school for any students who want to talk with them.

Winter Park High Principal Tim Smith, addressed the murders in a recorded call to students' homes asking families to speak with their children about the community's 'tragic loss'.

A candlelight vigil was held for the teenagers this evening at a bridge where their bodies were found.

Mourners left behind flowers, cards, and notes in loving memory of the friends.

Police are meanwhile trying to quell concerns in the community.

Nieves told WFTV: 'It appears to be isolated, but again, that's why it's so important for the community if they have any information, if there is any students out there.'
