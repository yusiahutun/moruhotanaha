Dick Clark’s New Year’s Rockin’ Eve was created by host and TV personality Dick Clark in 1972 as a younger competitor to musician Guy Lombardo’s popular and long-running New Year’s Eve special on CBS. The first Dick Clark’s New Year’s Rockin’ Eve special aired on December 31, 1972, on NBC, with Clark anchoring the coverage from Time Square in New York City.

Since then, Dick Clark’s New Year’s Rockin’ Eve has become the highest-rated New Year’s Eve special broadcast in the United States, with a peak of 22.6 million home viewers in 2012. Clark hosted Dick Clark’s New Year’s Rockin’ Eve from 1973 to 199, and from 2001 to 2004. He suffered a stroke in December 2004, weeks before the special was set to air, which resulted in Regis Philbin replacing him as a guest host. The stroke, which resulted in speech impediments, led Clark to cede his hosting duties to Ryan Seacrest the following year in 2005. Though Clark retired as the main host of the special, he continued to make limited appearance as a co-host until his death in 2012.

Throughout its 50-plus years on television, Dick Clark’s New Year’s Rockin’ Eve has featured hundreds of performers including Lady Gaga, Justin Bieber, BTS, Kesha, Taylor Swift, Miley cyrus, the Black Eyes Peas, Elton John, Jessica simpson, and more as viewers and hosts countdown to the annual ball drop in Time square. The special, which features both live and pre-recorded performances, also includes coverage of midnight festivities in other locations around the United States and the world, including Los Angeles, New Orleans, and San Juan.
