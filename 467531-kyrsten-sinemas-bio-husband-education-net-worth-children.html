Krysten was born on July 12, 1976, in Tucson, Arizona, the United States of America, to Marilyn (Wiley) and Dan Sinema. The American senator has two siblings, an elder brother and a younger sister.

Sinema's father, Dan, was a lawyer, just like her. Unfortunately, Krysten's parents separated when she was a child, and her mother remarried. Krysten's mother got full custody of the children and moved to DeFuniak Springs, Florida, a small town in the Panhandle.

Krysten's family faced a rough patch as they tried to make ends meet when she was young. Her stepfather lost his job, and the bank foreclosed their home. These hardships made them endure hard times for three years, even living in an abandoned gas station. Fortunately, they were back on their feet thanks to family, church, and hard work.

The American senator grew up as a member of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. She attended high school at Walton High School in DeFuniak Springs when she was 16. She later went to Brigham Young University, earning her Bachelor of Arts degree.

Krysten’s career

Of course, Kyrsten Sinema's education shaped her career as she taught at different institutions of higher learning. She started working as an adjunct professor teaching master’s level policy and grant-writing at Arizona State University School of Social Work. She was also an adjunct business law professor at Arizona Summit Law School, formerly Phoenix School of Law.

Krysten started her political career by joining Arizona Green Party before leaving it for the Arizona Democratic Party in 2004. The American politician ran as an independent candidate in 2001 and 2002 for local elected offices and lost.
