Football fans joke Leeds 'would fill' bonkers prototype stadium that holds one million

 Posted on June 13, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  383 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Leeds United supporters have been backed to fill a bonkers prototype stadium designed by American artist Paul Pfeiffer that could play host to one million spectators on a match day.

The concept art piece, which has been created in the shape of a huge bowl with 11 tiers of seating, is reminiscent of Barcelona's Nou Camp but more than 10x bigger than the 99,354 Catalan venue.

Twitter users were quick to think of Leeds when seeing the hypothetical art piece, poking fun at sections of their fan base who raved about their away following in the Championship and now in the Premier League.

One fan wrote: "Leeds United would fill it." With a second saying: "Leeds would take more."

A third then mirrored that by typing: "Leeds would have taken more." With a fourth agreeing: "Leeds would still take more."

The current largest stadium in the world is the Rungrado 1st of May Stadium in Pyongyang, North Korea - which can play host to 150,000.

Next is the Narendra Modi Stadium in Gujarat India, which can welcome 132,000 supporters and hosts India national cricket team fixtures.

This is the Michigan Stadium in the United States, which can bring in 107,601 for Michigan Wolverines colleague football matches.

Back in 2021, Leeds came fourth in a poll that ranked the fan base that creates the best atmosphere in the Premier League, and second in the list of noises away fans.

Elland Road then came in ninth regarding best stadiums to visit, but it was their travelling fans who earned plaudits from manager Jesse Marsch on the final say of the season.

He said: "I always love the energy and here at Leeds I’ve never felt energy like the supporters bring here. I don’t want them to chant my name, I want them to chant who we are."
