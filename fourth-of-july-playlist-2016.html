18 Best Patriotic 4th of July Songs For Your Independence Day Playlist

 Posted on May 16, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  750 words  |  Larita Shotwell

A holiday that ends in fireworks deserves a perfect party playlist to match. This Independence Day, we’re celebrating America’s birthday with some of our favorite crowd-pleasing “American” songs, both new and old. Whether you’re observing with a low-key picnic or a full-on rooftop party, consider your holiday soundtrack covered!

Check out our Fourth of July favorites below.

“American Girl” by Tom Petty and the Heartbreakers

No Fourth of July playlist is complete without Tom Petty & the Heartbreakers’ debut hit. As one of the greatest rock and roll bands of all time, they also know how to make a melodic pop classic that lasts all night.

“She’s American” by The 1975

On this catchy track off The 1975’s second album, singer Matty Healy points out some cultural differences from the bands experiences with female American fans. We get it. Our American Dream typically involves a handsome Brit prince, too.

“National Anthem” by Lana Del Rey

We couldn’t forget about Lana Del Rey’s epic “National Anthem,” music video in which she channels Jackie Kennedy and A$AP Rocky plays her JFK. All, rise.

“American Teen” by Khalid

There’s something so nostalgic about Khalid’s title track, though at 20 years old his teenage years aren’t too far behind. He sings: “’Cause this is our year/ So wake me up in the Spring/ While I’m high off my American dream.”

“American Boy” by Estelle feat. Kanye West

It’s hard to believe that this throwback jam featuring Yeezy came out ten years ago, but we’re bringing it back just in time for holiday celebrations.

“California Girls” by The Beach Boys

Whether you’re a hip East Coast girl or you grew up with the West Coast sunshine, the Beach Boys bring the perfect feel-good tunes for summer.

“American Music” by Violent Femmes

The way we see it, every playlist needs a quirky tune to spice things up. And this one, debating American music by the ‘80s cult band Violent Femmes, does the trick.

“Your Best American Girl” by Mitski

Equal parts heartbreaking and awe-inspiring, “Your Best American Girl” finds Mitski, a Japanese-American indie rocker, struggling in a relationship with someone from a different background. “Your mother wouldn't approve of how my mother raised me,” she sings in the chorus.

“Fireworks” by Drake feat. Alicia Keys

We’re still unsure if Drake’s anthem-meets-love song is about then-rumored girlfriend Rihanna, but the lyrics seem to offer a hint. He sings: “You never see it coming, you just get to see it go.... Now I can see it in her eyes, fireworks." But we’re bringing it back, because we still love it.

“This Is America” by Childish Gambino

The genius that is Childish Gambino, AKA Donald Glover, made waves with his latest release, in May, in which he draws attention to incessant gun violence in America. Don’t just listen; watch the video for full effect.

“American Dream” by LCD Soundsystem

The shimmering title track off of LCD Soundsystem’s rebirth of a last album has lead singer James Murphy reflecting on life and the reality of the American Dream. In the best way possible.

“Americans” by Janelle Monáe

Janelle Monáe’s third album, Dirty Computer, came out this April. While we still have “Make Me Feel” and “Pynk” on repeat, we want to draw your attention to her outstanding patriotic ode, which doesn’t glaze over real-life issues like racial tensions and gender inequality.

“American Dream” by Miami Horror

Miami Horror’s “American Dream” allows us a semi-patriotic song that you can dance to all night. The Australian indietronica band is influenced by new wave, pop punk, and hypnotic ‘80s pop. Dreamy.

“Young Americans” by David Bowie

Would any playlist be complete without a Bowie cameo? The answer is no.

“I Love The USA” by Weezer

This song was written by one of our favorite charismatic alt-rock groups of the ‘90s (and today) to honor NASA’s Juno Mission to Jupiter. How patriotic!

“American Money” by BØRNS

Garrett Clark Borns sings of something vaguely American on this track: a romanticized love, with the power to transcend time and space: “So take me to the paradise / It's in your eyes / Green like American money.”

“So American” by Portugal. The Man

This early track from the Alaskan indie band that brought us “Feel It Still” deserves a holiday listen. We’re still unsure if being “so American” is a compliment, but on the Fourth, we’re celebrating it. Cheers!
