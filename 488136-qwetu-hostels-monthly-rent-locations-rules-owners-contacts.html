Qwetu hostels monthly rent, locations, rules, owners, contacts

 Posted on September 5, 2024  |  6 minutes  |  1171 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Security and comfort are factors to consider when looking for accommodation, especially for students. Qwetu hostels are home to students from all walks of life who study at the universities and colleges in Nairobi. Their priority is to deliver the best possible living experience for students. The owners of this facility have thrived in ensuring top-notch services to their esteemed tenants and, in this case, students.

Qwetu hostels are among the well-known and sought-after classic student hostels and offer study space for students. The hostels are home to more than 4,300 students who study in universities and colleges within Nairobi.

About Qwetu hostels

Who is the owner of Qwetu hostels? The facility is owned by Edward Kirathe, the founder and chief executive officer of Acron holdings limited. He is an expert in the real estate business and has over twenty years of experience. The hostels were built as a joint enterprise between Acorn Holdings and Helios Investment Partners. Acorn Holdings is a UK-based private equity firm.

How many Qwetu residences are there?

At Qwetu you get to study, learn and connect with friends and peers. Qwetu is the only student accommodation provider in Kenya with residences that are fully equipped to host over 4,300 students. The facility has 8 residences as discussed below.

1. Qwetu WilsonView

This is home to a vibrant community of over 700 residents from various universities and colleges, including Riara University, Strathmore University, University of Nairobi, Daystar University and the Catholic University of Eastern Africa.

The hostel is located 5 minutes' walk from Strathmore University and 10 minutes' drive from Nairobi CBD. Qwetu Wilson hostel also nears T- Mall, Java Mbagathi Way and Wilson Airport.

2. Qwetu Aberdare Heights I

The hostel is located opposite Qwetu Aberdare Heights II and only 50 meters from the United States International University (USIU) campus and Pan African University. If you are after a home-away-from-home experience and luxury living, then Qwetu Aberdare Heights I is a good place to choose from.

3. Qwetu Aberdare Heights II

Qwetu Aberdare Heights II is home to the student community and is located 40 meters from the heart of the United States International University (USIU) campus and near Pan African University. Its proximity to both universities means taking the hustle out of your commute and saving you transport costs.

4. Qwetuu Jogoo hostel

This is the founding Qwetu residence, with its own kind of vibe. The hostel is home to over 500 residents from various universities and colleges, including the University of Nairobi (UON), Technical University (TUK), Strathmore and Daystar. It is located 20 minutes' drive from UON and 25 minutes to Nairobi CBD.

5.Qwetu Ruaraka hostel

Qwetu Ruaraka is the second Qwetu residence to join the Qwetu family, and it is located near Allsops, Ruaraka, off Thika Road, and near Garden City mall and TRM Mall. The hostel also hosts over 500 residents from various universities and colleges, including Africa Nazarene, USIU, KCA University, Kenyatta University, Zetech University and Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology.

6. Qwetu Parklands hostel

Qwetu parklands hostel is located just 10 minutes away from the CBD. It is ideal for students studying near the Nairobi CBD, like UON, Technical University of Kenya and Mount Kenya University (Nairobi campus).

7. Qwetu Hurlingham

It is located along Argwings Kodhek Road, a short walk to Daystar University and a 10-minute drive from Strathmore University and Nairobi CBD. The hostel can accommodate more than 700 students.

8. Qwetu Karen

Qwetu Karen is located 2 minutes' walk from the Catholic University of East Africa. The hostel is home to more than 600 students from various universities and colleges, including Africa Nazarene University, JKUAT Karen, Co-operative University, Africa International University, Kenya School of Law and Multimedia University.

How much is rent at Qwetu?

Qwetu hostels' prices per month vary depending on the room type, facility packages and other value-added services. Here are the prices for their rooms.

  • Twin room - Ksh-17,500 single bed

Qwetu hostels rules

Qwetu management has developed rules that govern every member that stays within the premises. These rules help the students to know what is expected of them at all times. The rules and regulations are a way of providing safety to the students. What are these rules?

  • Alert your customer service team whether you are alone or with someone at the time of application.
  • Illegal substances are prohibited.
  • Defacing walls and furniture is strictly prohibited.
  • Stealing from other students is not allowed.
  • Misuse and destruction of property are not accepted.
  • Pay your rent within the time frame agreed in the hostel policy.

It is good to note that before you secure a room at Qwetu, you are to provide valid proof that you are currently enrolled as a student and are taking a course or courses at a given institution. An applicant is also required to be free of any offences that are punishable by the laws of Kenya.

Are guests allowed at Qwetu?

It is possible to invite a friend to Qwetu hostels depending on the availability at the time of the reservation. Technically Qwetu hostels can accommodate only two people to sleep in one bed but still pay for it. Every guest also gets one single bed. There is no capacity for extra beds at Qwetu hostels.
