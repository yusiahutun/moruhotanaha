Top 5 Things to Do When Locked Out of Your Home

 Posted on August 5, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  528 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Getting locked out of your home is one of the worst experiences ever. You’re probably tired from a long day of work, and you’re faced with such an exasperating situation. You’re probably close to panicking, not knowing what you’re supposed to do in such a scenario. It can indeed be a trying period, but you don’t have to fret. There are several things you can do to resolve the problem quickly. 

You only have to keep your wits about you. Most people who find themselves in such a scenario tend to be nervous, affecting their ability to focus on the task at hand. You must consider several alternatives to ensure you will get through the ordeal as quickly as possible. 

Here are some things you can do when you’re locked out of your home.

1. Check for any unlocked doors or windows

Typically, don’t panic when you cannot open the front door. One of the best ways to resolve the problem is to look for unlocked windows and doors which you can use to gain access to your house. Try not to get stuck in small openings such as the chimney or a bathroom window. It will only make the ordeal worse. 

2. Call a Professional

One of the fastest ways to get through a lockout problem is by calling a locksmith local to your area. First, however, you must work with reputable locksmith San Diego companies which have been fully vetted providing affordable and efficient services to their clientele. Calling them on their hotlines will help homemakers get through the situation fast and securely. 

3. Break out the card

If you have a card, such as a membership or an old credit card, that you’re no longer using, you can use them to unlock the door. The process is pretty simple, as you only need to put the card between the door and the frame. Then, once you’ve jimmied the card in place, you must push the card away from the knob towards the frame as you try to undo the latch. However, note that this would only work if your door is a standard-type lock and not a deadbolt. 

4. Call the landlord 

If you live in an apartment complex, one of the things you must consider doing first is calling the landlord or the front desk associate. They most probably have a spare key that you can use to gain entry to your unit. If that’s the case, after opening the door, you can either request a lock replacement or a rekey service to ensure that your unit will be secure.

5. Break the door knob

If you’re in a hurry to get inside the house, no matter the consequences, you might consider breaking the doorknob outright. All you need to do is to hit the knob and break it off from the rest of the mechanism. However, if you do this, it will be best to call a locksmith immediately to replace the broken lock. 

Conclusion 

If you find yourself locked out of your home, it’s best not to fret. There are several ways that you can gain entry to your property easily.
