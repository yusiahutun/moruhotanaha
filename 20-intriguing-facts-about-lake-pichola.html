Lake Pichola is a picturesque artificial lake located in Udaipur, Rajasthan.

Situated in the heart of the city, Lake Pichola is one of the most prominent attractions in Udaipur, known for its stunning beauty and historical significance.

The lake was created in the 14th century and has a rich history.

Lake Pichola was created by Maharana Udai Singh II, the founder of Udaipur, to provide a source of water for the city and its inhabitants.

Lake Pichola covers an area of approximately 696 hectares.

With its vast expanse, Lake Pichola offers a serene and tranquil atmosphere for visitors to enjoy.

The lake is surrounded by several magnificent palaces and historical landmarks.

One of the most popular attractions on Lake Pichola is the Lake Palace, a beautiful marble palace that appears to float on the water.

The lake offers breathtaking views of the surrounding Aravalli Mountains.

Visitors can witness the stunning beauty of the Aravalli range while gazing at the crystal-clear waters of Lake Pichola.

Lake Pichola is a popular destination for boat rides and cruises.

Exploring the lake by boat is a must-do activity for visitors, providing a unique perspective of the surrounding landmarks and vibrant cityscape.

The lake is home to several species of birds and aquatic life.

Nature enthusiasts can spot a variety of birds, including migratory species, and witness the lively ecosystem thriving within the lake.

Lake Pichola played a significant role in the defense of Udaipur.

During times of war, the lake acted as a natural barrier, protecting the city from enemy invasions.

The iconic Gangaur Festival is celebrated on the banks of Lake Pichola.

During this vibrant festival, locals gather near the lake to offer prayers and participate in processions.

The lake has been featured in several Bollywood movies.

Lake Pichola’s ethereal beauty has captivated filmmakers, making it a popular shooting location for Indian cinema.

The water level in Lake Pichola is regulated by a sluice gate.

The sluice gate helps in maintaining a consistent water level and prevents overflow during heavy rainfall.

The lake is named after the nearby Picholi village.

The village of Picholi, located on the eastern shore of the lake, lends its name to this magnificent waterbody.

Lake Pichola offers stunning sunset views.

Witnessing the vibrant hues of the sunset reflecting on the tranquil waters of the lake is a mesmerizing experience.

Boat races are organized on Lake Pichola during special occasions.

The boat races showcase the traditional Rajasthani culture and attract both locals and tourists.

The lake is surrounded by lush green gardens and parks.

Enjoy a leisurely stroll through the beautiful gardens that adorn the banks of Lake Pichola.

The famous Jag Mandir palace is located on an island in Lake Pichola.

Jag Mandir, also known as the Lake Garden Palace, offers a majestic view of the lake and serves as a luxurious venue for events and weddings.

The lake transforms into a magical spectacle during festivals and celebrations.

During Diwali and other major festivals, the lake is beautifully illuminated with countless lights, creating a magical ambiance.

Lake Pichola’s beauty has inspired poets, artists, and writers throughout history.

Its serene surroundings and enchanting views have provided ample inspiration for creative minds.

The lake is a popular spot for photographers to capture stunning landscapes and reflections.

Professional and amateur photographers flock to Lake Pichola to capture its timeless beauty through their lenses.

Lake Pichola remains a symbol of Udaipur’s rich cultural heritage and architectural marvels.

It continues to captivate visitors from around the world, showcasing the grandeur and charm of Rajasthan’s royal history.

FAQs

1. How big is Lake Pichola?

Lake Pichola is approximately 4 kilometers long and 3 kilometers wide. It covers an area of about 6 square kilometers.

2. Can you swim in Lake Pichola?

No, swimming is not allowed in Lake Pichola due to safety reasons. The lake is quite deep and there are also boat traffic and other hazards that make swimming dangerous.

3. What are the major attractions near Lake Pichola?

There are several major attractions near Lake Pichola, including the City Palace, Jag Mandir, Jagdish Temple, and the Bagore Ki Haveli museum. These attractions showcase the rich history and architecture of Udaipur.

4. How old is Lake Pichola?

Lake Pichola was built in the year 1362 AD by a local ruler named Maharana Udai Singh II. It was initially created as a water reservoir to cater to the needs of the city.

5. Can I take a boat ride on Lake Pichola?

Absolutely! Boat rides are a popular way to explore Lake Pichola. You can choose from various types of boat rides, including motorboats, speedboats, and traditional wooden boats. It’s an excellent way to admire the stunning views of the lake and its surroundings.
