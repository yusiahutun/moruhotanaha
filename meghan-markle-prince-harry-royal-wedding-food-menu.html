Royal Wedding Food Guide - Meghan Markle and Prince Harry's Wedding Menu

 Posted on May 20, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  801 words  |  Larita Shotwell

We now know it all–from what Meghan Markle wore to wed Prince Harry to the ins and outs of the ceremony proceedings. But it's not over yet; we need to know it all–from the décor to the culinary details. Given that Meghan used to pen a lifestyle blog with a focus on food (the blog was called The Tig, named after the full-bodied Tuscan red varietal, Tignanello), we were dying to know what the couple chose to serve their guests–which ranged from big names like Oprah Winfrey and George and Amal Clooney, to Idris Elba and their royal relatives.

Town & Country reports, per The Telegraph, that Harry and Meghan landed on a caterer this past January. Reportedly, the couple tapped catering company Table Talk, a royal favorite, for their wedding day. The company was responsible for Will and Kate's wedding reception in 2011, and also catered Pippa Middleton and James Matthews's wedding this past spring.

The royal wedding general reception, which took place at noon in Windsor, will be followed by an evening reception hosted at Frogmore House by Prince Charles and Camilla Parker Bowles. The luncheon, which was hosted by the Queen at Windsor Castle, featured a lighter menu suitable for a warm weather afternoon.

According to Delish, guests at the Queen's luncheon snacked on:

  • Scottish Langoustines wrapped in Smoked Salmon with Citrus Crème Fraiche
  • Grilled English Asparagus wrapped in Cumbrian Ham
  • Garden Pea Panna Cotta with Quail Eggs and Lemon Verbena
  • Heritage Tomato and Basil Tartare with Balsamic Pearls
  • Poached Free Range Chicken bound in a Lightly Spiced Yoghurt with Roasted Apricot
  • Croquette of Confit Windsor Lamb, Roasted Vegetables and Shallot Jam
  • Warm Asparagus Spears with Mozzarella and Sun-Blush Tomatoes

Getty Images

As for the trendy and more filling bowls being passed, guests dined on:

  • Fricassee of Free Range Chicken with Morel Mushrooms and Young Leeks
  • Pea and Mint Risotto with Pea Shoots, Truffle Oil and Parmesan Crisps
  • Ten Hour Slow Roasted Windsor Pork Belly with Apple Compote and Crackling

T0 pair with the lemon elderflower cake by Claire Ptak of Violet Cakes, there were Champagne and Pistachio Macaroons, Orange Crème Brûlée Tartlets, and Miniature Rhubarb Crumble Tartlets. Pol Roger Brut Réserve Champagne, a selection of wines, and an apple elderflower mocktail were also served.

For the evening reception hosted by Prince Charles featuring a seated, three-course meal, Harry and Meghan's evening affair will likely feature the most formal fare.

People reports that Royal chef Mark Flanagan is leading the charge in the kitchen, as the Palace played host to 600 guests for lunch, and around 200 for dinner. According to reports, Meghan and Harry visited the kitchens at Windsor Castle to plan the menu, which features seasonal ingredients sourced from the the Queen's estates. The menu featured passed canapés and more hearty bowls of food so that guests can enjoy their meals standing up, making it easier for the newlyweds to greet their guests and mingle amongst the crowd.

"There’s no experimentation on Saturday whatsoever, tried and tested and predominantly classics,” Flanagan told reporters. With that in mind, guests were treated to seasonal British produce like asparagus, peas and tomatoes.

In contrast, Will and Kate's wedding dinner, according to their menu which was up for auction in 2015, featured an appetizer of marinated salmon, crab and langoustines on a fresh herb salad, followed by organic lamb served with spring vegetables, potatoes and "sauce Windsor," finished with a trio of desserts: Berkshire honey ice cream, sherry trifle and a chocolate parfait. Each dish was appropriately paired with wine, and coffee and fresh mint tea were offered with dessert.

Nate D. Sanders Auctions

Given Meghan's love for bold, red wines, guests at dinner will likely be treated to a menu and wine pairings fit for foodies. And, while Will and Kate's menu featured only one red wine paired with their lamb course, we wouldn't be surprised if Meghan and Harry choose to offer more of an array of wines, along with a full bar, for their guests this evening.

Ptak presented a roughly-iced cake in buttercream this afternoon, with the tiers deconstructed, and decorated it with fresh flowers fit for a spring wedding. Violet Cakes tapped into the blooms that florist Phillippa Craddock used to decorate the Cathedral, Castle and Frogmore House when adorning Harry and Meghan's confection.

As for who is footing the bill for the evening, the statement from Kensington Palace confirmed that the Royal Family will take care of the core wedding expenses, which includes the food and beverage tab.
